💡 How the "STR Tax Loophole" Works

Under Section 469 of the Internal Revenue Code, rental activities are typically considered passive by default, meaning losses cannot offset active income like W-2 wages. However, an exception exists if the average guest stay is 7 days or less. Under these conditions, the property is classified as a business rather than a rental activity, freeing it from the passive loss rules, provided you materially participate in the operations.

⏱️ Material Participation Tests You must meet at least one of these tests (the most common are):
• 500 hours: Spend 500+ hours on the activity.
• 100 hours + most: Spend 100+ hours, and more than any other single person (including cleaning crews/property managers).
• Sole participant: You do substantially all the work.
🛡️ Audit Defense & Log Prep Because this loophole is highly lucrative, it is heavily scrutinized by the IRS. To protect your deductions, you should maintain a contemporaneous time log showing the date, duration, and details of every task you perform (e.g., messaging guests, staging, maintenance, purchasing supplies).
